Hi, I'm

Nikhil Bisht

a Grad Student @ Dept. Of Physics, FSU, Florida

I am working towards building spatiotemporal Physics Informed Neural Network Models that can be used for forecasting complex and non-linear fluid dynamics systems. My application of these models is to predict locations of Star Formation in the highly inhomogeneous local neighbourhood in our galaxy where turbulence and chaos causes molecular clouds to fragment and gravitationally collapse to become stellar nurseries and ultimately form stars. Other applications of these models can be in weather forecasting, solar radiation forecasting and etc.

About Me

I am

a graduate student at Florida State University, working with Prof. David Collins on Simulating Molecular Cloud Collapse using ENZO , an adaptive mesh refinement simulation code designed for rich, multi-physics hydrodynamic astrophysical calculations. The aim is to understand better when, where and how gas collapses to form pre-stellar cores and how they evolve to form the stars we see today.
