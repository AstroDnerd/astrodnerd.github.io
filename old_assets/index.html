About Me

I'm a computational astrophysicist studying how turbulence and magnetic fields govern the collapse of molecular clouds and set the stage for star formation. My work couples high-resolution magnetized and turbulent cloud collapse simulations using the code ENZO with spatiotemporal machine learning models to forecast where and when dense, gravitationally bound cores appear. With a background in Computer Science and Physics, I sit at the intersection of numerical hydrodynamics and data-driven modeling and am interested in both academic collaborations and industry research opportunities in hydrodynamics or machine learning.

When I'm not training models or running simulations I spend with my cats and chickens, chase the night sky with astrophotography, and go exploring in the real world by biking, hiking, and camping whenever I can or in the fantasy world by running DnD campaigns.

Selected Projects & Research

Simulating Molecular Cloud Collapse with ENZO

Using ENZO with AMR to model self-gravitating turbulent molecular clouds, including sink particles and feedback, to study when and where stars form.

Machine Learning for Star Formation

Building 3D spatiotemporal models (2.1M-parameter UNet + Conv-GRU) to forecast collapse from multi-field MHD simulation datasets using physics-aware training & evaluation.

Stellar Radial Migration (TNG50)

Master's thesis project analysing radial migration of stars across 198 MW/M31 analogs in TNG50 simulation where I measured typical migration rates and trends with galaxy morphology.
